About PhytoSynergyDB

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) is one of the greatest threats to global health. The ESKAPE pathogens — Enterococcus faecium, Staphylococcus aureus, Klebsiella pneumoniae, Acinetobacter baumannii, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and Enterobacter spp. — are responsible for the majority of hospital-acquired infections worldwide.

PhytoSynergyDB is the first curated database dedicated to cataloguing synergistic interactions between phytochemicals (plant-derived compounds) and conventional antibiotics against these critical pathogens. Each entry includes MIC values, FIC indices, interpretation, and observed mechanisms of action, all extracted from peer-reviewed literature.

Our goal is to accelerate the discovery of combination therapies that can restore the efficacy of existing antibiotics and provide researchers with a centralized, structured resource for antimicrobial synergy data.

Our Mission

PhytoSynergyDB aims to bridge the gap between phytochemistry and clinical antimicrobial research. By providing a centralized, curated repository of synergy data, we empower researchers worldwide to discover new combination therapies against the most dangerous drug-resistant pathogens.
