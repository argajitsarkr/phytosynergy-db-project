About PhytoSynergyDB

The Problem

Antimicrobial resistance (AMR) is a growing global health crisis. The World Health Organization has declared AMR one of the top ten threats to global public health. The ESKAPE pathogens - Enterococcus faecium, Staphylococcus aureus, Klebsiella pneumoniae, Acinetobacter baumannii, Pseudomonas aeruginosa, and Enterobacter spp. - are responsible for the majority of hospital-acquired infections and are increasingly resistant to conventional antibiotics. New therapeutic strategies are urgently needed.

Our Approach

PhytoSynergyDB addresses this challenge by systematically curating data from peer-reviewed scientific literature on synergistic interactions between plant-derived phytochemicals and conventional antibiotics. Combination therapy - using a phytochemical to potentiate the activity of an existing antibiotic - represents a promising strategy to overcome resistance. Our database provides a centralized, structured resource to accelerate this research.

Scope & Inclusion Criteria

PhytoSynergyDB indexes only experimental records that satisfy the following criteria:

  • Defined compounds. The phytochemical must be a structurally characterised molecule with at least a name and ideally a PubChem CID, SMILES or InChIKey. Crude plant extracts without an identified active component are excluded.
  • Quantitative combination data. Studies must report MIC values for the phytochemical alone, the antibiotic alone, and the combination, using checkerboard, broth-microdilution, time-kill, or disk-diffusion assays.
  • ESKAPE pathogens. At least one tested organism must belong to the ESKAPE group; pathogen genus, species and strain are recorded where reported.
  • Peer-reviewed primary sources. Each record cites a journal article identified by DOI or PMID. Conference abstracts, theses and unindexed preprints are excluded.
  • In vitro focus. The current release indexes in vitro interaction data; in vivo and clinical evidence are out of scope for now.

Data Model

The relational schema is centred on a SynergyExperiment record that links the six surrounding entities. A composite uniqueness constraint on (phytochemical, antibiotic, pathogen, source) prevents duplicate insertion.

Phytochemical

Compound name, PubChem CID, SMILES, InChIKey, MW, XLogP, HBD/HBA, TPSA, ring count, Lipinski violations, ChemOnt taxonomy.

Antibiotic

Name, DrugBank ID, antibiotic class (mechanistic grouping).

Pathogen

Genus, species, strain, Gram stain. Unique on (genus, species, strain).

Source

DOI, PMID, journal, publication year, article title.

Plant

Scientific name, common name, family. Many-to-many link to phytochemicals (botanical provenance).

SynergyExperiment

Four MIC values, units, FIC index, interpretation, assay method, observed mechanism of action, curator notes.

Methodology & Data Standards

All data in PhytoSynergyDB is manually extracted from peer-reviewed publications through a rigorous curation process and is then enriched automatically with chemistry annotations.

Literature Search

Systematic search of PubMed, Scopus, and Web of Science for studies reporting phytochemical-antibiotic combination effects against ESKAPE pathogens using checkerboard assays or similar quantitative methods.

Data Extraction

Manual extraction of MIC values (alone and in combination), FIC indices, synergy interpretations, pathogen strain details, and observed mechanisms of action from each qualifying publication.

FIC Calculation

Fractional Inhibitory Concentration (FIC) index is calculated as: FIC = (MICphyto,combo / MICphyto,alone) + (MICabx,combo / MICabx,alone). Interpretation follows standard thresholds.

Quality Control

Each record links to its original source via DOI, ensuring full traceability and reproducibility. Data undergoes validation before inclusion. Chemical identifiers (PubChem CID, InChI Key, SMILES) ensure compound disambiguation.

Automated Chemical Enrichment

Every new phytochemical is annotated through the PubChem PUG-REST API (canonical SMILES, InChIKey, molecular weight, XLogP, HBD/HBA, TPSA, rotatable bonds) and the ClassyFire service (three-level ChemOnt taxonomy: superclass, class, subclass). Curator-supplied fields are never overwritten.

Local Cheminformatics (RDKit)

RDKit 2024.3.1 computes logP (Wildman-Crippen), TPSA, ring count, heavy-atom count and Lipinski Rule-of-Five compliance. Compounds with at most one violation are flagged as drug-like for downstream filtering.

Synergy Interpretation Standards

FIC Index Range Interpretation Description
FIC ≤ 0.5 Synergy The combination is significantly more effective than either agent alone
0.5 < FIC ≤ 1.0 Additive The combination effect equals the sum of individual effects
1.0 < FIC ≤ 4.0 Indifference The combination has no significant interaction effect
FIC > 4.0 Antagonism The combination is less effective than individual agents

Limitations

  • Publication bias. Synergistic outcomes are reported more often than indifference or antagonism, so observed proportions over-represent favourable interactions.
  • Protocol heterogeneity. Inoculum size, growth medium and incubation time vary between primary studies; absolute MICs are therefore not strictly comparable across sources even when units agree.
  • Endpoint-only interpretation. The current release relies on MIC-based FIC indices; time-kill kinetics and isobologram analyses are out of scope.
  • In vitro evidence only. Pharmacokinetics, bioavailability and host-pathogen factors are not captured.
  • Curation lag. Newly published literature takes time to enter the database; users are encouraged to check the original DOI for the latest interpretation.

How to Cite

If you use PhytoSynergyDB in your research, please cite the resource:

Suggested citation: Sarkar A. et al. PhytoSynergyDB: A Curated Database of Phytochemical-Antibiotic Synergies Against ESKAPE Pathogens. v1.0 (2026). Available at: https://phytosynergydb.in. Accessed: {{ last_curation_date }}.

If you reuse derived chemical descriptors, please also cite the underlying chemistry sources:

  • PubChem: Kim S. et al. PubChem 2023 update. Nucleic Acids Research 51(D1):D1373-D1380 (2023).
  • ClassyFire / ChemOnt: Djoumbou Feunang Y. et al. ClassyFire: automated chemical classification with a comprehensive, computable taxonomy. J. Cheminform. 8:61 (2016).
  • RDKit: Landrum G. et al. RDKit: Open-source cheminformatics, release 2024.03.1.
